## Task 2 - Interview Questions & Answers

---

### 1. What is phishing?  
Phishing is a type of cyberattack where attackers send fake emails pretending to be from trusted companies. The goal is to steal sensitive data like login credentials, financial information, or personal identity.

---

### 2. How to identify a phishing email?  
You can spot a phishing email by checking for:
- Suspicious sender addresses  
- Urgent or threatening language  
- Generic greetings like “Dear Customer”  
- Spelling or grammar mistakes  
- Suspicious links (hover to check actual destination)  
- Unexpected attachments

---

### 3. What is email spoofing?  
Email spoofing is when attackers forge the sender's email address to make it appear as if the email is coming from someone you know or trust, like a bank or service provider.

---

### 4. Why are phishing emails dangerous?  
Phishing emails can:
- Steal sensitive data  
- Spread malware  
- Lead to financial loss  
- Enable identity theft  
- Compromise accounts or systems

---

### 5. How can you verify the sender’s authenticity?  
- Check the full email address, not just the display name  
- Hover over any links (don’t click) to verify their destination  
- Use an email header analyzer to verify where the message came from  
- Contact the company directly using official websites

---

### 6. What tools can analyze email headers?  
- Google’s Message Header Analyzer  
- MXToolbox  
- Mail Header Analyzer  
These tools reveal the real sender’s IP address and domain path.

---

### 7. What actions should be taken on suspected phishing emails?  
- Do not click any links or download attachments  
- Report it to your IT/security team  
- Mark it as phishing in your email platform  
- Delete the message

---

### 8. How do attackers use social engineering in phishing?  
Attackers manipulate emotions like fear, urgency, or curiosity to make you act quickly without thinking.  
Example: “Your account will be locked in 1 hour! Click here to fix it.”
